Bubat War, Fire Triggering Java vs Sunda Sentiment
Have you ever heard of this myth: Javanese are forbidden to marry Sundanese? Did you know, before 2010, there were no street names Pajajaran & Siliwangi in Surabaya and no name of Gajah Mada & Hayam Wuruk in Bandung? Apparently this was triggered by a phenomenal war between the Majapahit Kingdom of East Java and the Pajajaran Kingdom from West Java called the Bubat war.

The Bubat War event began with the intentions of Prabu Hayam Wuruk who wanted to marry the daughter of Dyah Pitaloka Citraresmi from the Sunda Country. It is said that Hayam Wuruk’s interest in the princess was due to the circulation of the painting of the princess in Majapahit, which was painted secretly by an artist at that time, named Sungging Prabangkara (somewhat similar to the early part of Sultan Ahmet & Kösem Sultan).

The intention of the marriage was to strengthen the long-broken brotherhood between Majapahit and Sunda. Raden Wijaya who was the founder of the Majapahit kingdom was considered Sundanese from Dyah Lembu Tal and her husband, Rakeyan Jayadarma, king of the Sunda kingdom. This is also recorded in the Rajya Rajya i Bhumi Nusantara Library parwa II sarga 3. In the Babad Tanah Jawi, Raden Wijaya is also called the name Jaka Susuruh from Pajajaran. Nevertheless, the Pajajaran historical record is considered to be weak in its truth, especially because the name Dyah Lembu Tal is a male name.

The general reason that could be accepted was Hayam Wuruk did intend to marry Dyah Pitaloka with political reasons, namely to bind alliance with the Kingdom of Sunda.Upon the blessing of the Majapahit royal family, Hayam Wuruk sent a letter of honor to Maharaja Linggabuana to apply for Dyah Pitaloka. The wedding ceremony will be held at Majapahit. The Sunda State royal council itself actually objected, especially Mangkubumi Hyang Bunisora ​​Suradipati.

Linggabuana decided to leave for Majapahit, because of the existing sense of brotherhood from the two country’s ancestral lines. Linggabuana departed with the Sunda group to Majapahit and was accepted and placed in Pesanggrahan Bubat.The Sunda king came to Bubat along with the empress and daughter of Dyah Pitaloka accompanied by a few soldiers. According to Kidung Sundayana, Mahapatih Gajah Mada’s intention arose to control the Kingdom of Sunda. Gajah Mada wanted to fulfill the Palapa Oath he made before Hayam Wuruk ascended the throne, because of the various kingdoms in the archipelago that had been conquered by Majapahit, only the Sunda kingdom had not been mastered.

With this intention, Gajah Mada made an excuse by assuming that the arrival of the Sundanese group in Pesanggrahan Bubat was a form of surrender of the Sunda Kingdom to Majapahit. Gajah Mada urged Hayam Wuruk to accept Dyah Pitaloka not as a bride, but as a sign of surrender to the Sundanese country and recognition of Majapahit’s superiority over Sundanese in the archipelago. Hayam Wuruk himself was said to be hesitant about the problem, given that Gajah Mada was the Mahapatih that Majapahit relied on at the time.

Then there was an incident of a dispute between the Linggabuana envoy and Gajah Mada. This dispute ended with the cursing of Gajah Mada by the Sunda State envoy who was surprised that their arrival was only to give a sign of submission and recognize Majapahit’s superiority, not because of the previous invitation. But Gajah Mada remained in its original position.Not to mention Hayam Wuruk gave his decision, Gajah Mada had deployed his troops (Bhayangkara) to Pesanggrahan Bubat and threatened Linggabuana to recognize Majapahit’s superiority. In order to maintain the honor of being a Sundanese knight, Linggabuana rejected the pressure. An unbalanced battle ensued between Gajah Mada and his large army, against Linggabuana with a small royal guard (Balamati) and royal officials and ministers who participated in the visit.The incident ended with the death of Linggabuana, ministers, royal officials and the entire Sundanese royal family. The Sundanese king and all Sundanese royal officials can be brought in Majapahit and perish on the Bubat field

Putri Dyah Pitaloka, who is still alive, then defends starch, commits suicide to defend the honor of her nation and country. According to the behavior and ksatriya caste values, ritual suicide was carried out by the women of the caste if the men had died. The act is expected to defend self-esteem as well as to protect their sanctity, which is to face the possibility of being humiliated because of rape, persecution, or enslaving.As a result of the Bubat incident, it was said in the note that Hayam Wuruk’s relationship with Gajah Mada was tenuous. Gajah Mada himself faced opposition, suspicion and criticism from the officials and nobility of Majapahit, because his actions were considered careless and rash. He is considered too brave and presumptuous by not heeding King Hayam Wuruk’s own wishes and feelings.

This misfortune event also marked the start of Gajah Mada’s career, because then Hayam Wuruk awarded him a private land in Madakaripura (Probolinggo). Although this action appears to be an award, this action can be interpreted as a subtle suggestion so that Gajah Mada begins to consider retirement, because this land is far from the capital city of Majapahit so Gajah Mada began to resign from the political state of the Majapahit court.This tragedy damaged the state relations between the two kingdoms and continued for many years later, the relationship between Sunda-Majapahit never harmonized.

The act of courage and dignity of the Sunda King and daughter of Dyah Pitaloka to carry out the act of defending the star (daring to die) was respected and glorified by the Sundanese people and considered as an example. Raja Lingga Buana was nicknamed “Prabu Wangi” because of his heroic defense of his country’s pride. His descendants, the Sunda kings were then dubbed Siliwangi which came from the word Silih Wangi which means successor, heir or successor to Prabu Wangi.
